About this map. Organised strictly by a personal taxonomy — three branches (Matter / Form / Connection), 21 disciplines — not conventional academic categories. The unconventional placement is the point. Each card carries the year, the person(s), their institution and country, and one line on why it mattered. Dashed arrows show "builds on → led to" lineage within and across branches.
Links & honesty. Where a free full text is reliably available it's linked directly (arXiv, PubMed Central, W3C, APS open letters, author/lab pages). Otherwise every card opens pre-filtered searches on OpenAlex, CORE, Semantic Scholar and Google Scholar — these always resolve to current free results, so you never hit a dead link. "Latest research" buttons open live field feeds (arXiv, bioRxiv, PsyArXiv, SocArXiv, ChemRxiv, PhilPapers, RePEc). A few older or paywalled landmark works link to search rather than a single PDF on purpose — better an honest search than a fabricated link.
Refreshing. A static file can't update its own curated "recent breakthroughs." To refresh the curated content, re-run the prompt that generated this file and I'll rebuild it with newer milestones. The "Latest research" buttons, by contrast, are always live.
Install it as an app. Once hosted, use your browser's "Install app" / "Add to Home Screen" option to launch it fullscreen and offline.
