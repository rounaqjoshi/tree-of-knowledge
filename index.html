The Tree of Knowledge

A map of human discovery — Matter · Form · Connection
About this map. Organised strictly by a personal taxonomy — three branches (Matter / Form / Connection), 21 disciplines — not conventional academic categories. The unconventional placement is the point. Each card carries the year, the person(s), their institution and country, and one line on why it mattered. Dashed threads show "builds on → led to" lineage within and across branches.
Links & honesty. Where a free full text is reliably available it's linked directly (arXiv, PubMed Central, W3C, APS open letters, author/lab pages). Otherwise every card opens pre-filtered searches on OpenAlex, CORE, Semantic Scholar and Google Scholar — these always resolve to current free results, so you never hit a dead link. "Latest research" links open live field feeds (arXiv, bioRxiv, PsyArXiv, SocArXiv, ChemRxiv, PhilPapers, RePEc).
Refreshing. A static file can't update its own curated "recent breakthroughs." To refresh the curated content, re-run the prompt that generated this file and it will rebuild with newer milestones. The "Latest research" links, by contrast, are always live.
